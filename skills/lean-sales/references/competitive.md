## 2. Displacement Strategy Development

### Winning Against Incumbent Solutions
```
DISPLACEMENT PLAYBOOK
1. Root Cause Analysis
   - Why customer chose competitor initially
   - Unmet needs or new pain points
   - Cost/performance misalignment

2. Differentiation Narrative
   - 2-3 compelling advantages
   - Quantified ROI vs. incumbent
   - Customer success proof points

3. Transition Risk Mitigation
   - Data migration roadmap
   - Training and onboarding plan
   - Parallel run scenario (if applicable)
   - Guaranteed outcome timeline

4. Economic Justification
   - True cost of ownership (TCO) analysis
   - Quick wins in first 30/60/90 days
   - Payback period calculation
   - Ongoing efficiency gains

5. Executive Sponsorship
   - Identify champion department
   - Build business case with CFO
   - Quantify team productivity gains
```

### Displacement Triggers to Exploit
- Major contract renewal cycles
- Key personnel changes in prospect organization
- Competitor price increases or feature cuts
- Known platform limitations (scalability, integration gaps)
- Poor customer service incidents (public complaints)
- Announced end-of-life for competitor product

## 6. Defensive Positioning Strategies

### Protecting Your Customer Base
```
DEFENSE PLAYBOOK
1. Customer Health Monitoring
   ├── NPS/CSAT tracking
   ├── Usage analytics and engagement
   ├── Support ticket sentiment
   ├── ROI realization metrics
   └── Competitive threat indicators

2. Proactive Engagement
   ├── Executive business reviews (QBR)
   ├── Product roadmap transparency
   ├── Feature request prioritization
   ├── Expansion opportunity identification
   └── Industry thought leadership

3. Switching Cost Management
   ├── Data integration deepening
   ├── Workflow customization
   ├── Team training and certification
   ├── Custom integrations (strategic accounts)
   └── Contractual protections

4. Competitive Countermeasure
   ├── Rapid feature response deployment
   ├── Price matching discretion
   ├── Extended trial or POC terms
   ├── Loyalty incentives or discounts
   └── Customer references for objection handling
```

### Red Flag Monitoring
Watch for these signals in customer interactions:
- Reduced usage or engagement
- Delayed renewals or contract negotiations
- New stakeholders entering procurement
- Competitive RFP mentions
- Reduced support interaction volume
- Escalated technical issues without resolution

## 8. Competitive Response Playbook

### Immediate Response Protocol
```
COMPETITIVE THREAT RESPONSE TIMELINE

WHEN COMPETITIVE THREAT DETECTED (Hour 0)
├── Verify threat authenticity
├── Assess deal size and strategic importance
├── Identify customer pain points driving comparison
├── Review competitive intelligence files
└── Alert sales leadership and product

HOUR 0-4: QUICK ASSESSMENT
├── Pull relevant win/loss analysis
├── Gather latest battle cards
├── Identify customer reference who faced similar decision
├── Prepare value comparison spreadsheet
└── Schedule sales leadership call

HOUR 4-8: STRATEGY CALL
├── Determine if we should compete or walk
├── Identify key objections to preempt
├── Select proof points and messaging
├── Assign customer reference intro
└── Plan next customer interaction timing

HOUR 8-24: CUSTOMER ENGAGEMENT
├── Deliver strategic value conversation
├── Address economic differential
├── Introduce relevant customer reference
├── Reframe decision timeline if favorable
└── Clarify next steps and decision criteria

DAY 2-7: EXECUTION PHASE
├── Execute reference call
├── Deliver tailored pricing/proposal if needed
├── Host product demo of key differentiators
├── Provide implementation roadmap
└── Maintain momentum cadence

POST-DECISION
├── Win/loss analysis documentation
├── Battle card and objection handler updates
├── Win share distributed to sales team
└── Customer reference cultivation (if won)
```

### Competitive Playbook By Scenario
```
SCENARIO: Customer in proof-of-concept phase
Action: Accelerate customer success, quantify ROI daily
Risk: Competitor validates capability match

SCENARIO: During renewal negotiation
Action: Expand scope before competitor can block
Risk: Customer used as price compression leverage

SCENARIO: Lost deal, customer open to reconsideration
Action: Identify changed circumstances or new pain points
Risk: Relationship further erodes if forced approach

SCENARIO: Major customer received competitor offer
Action: Rapid QBR to assess satisfaction level
Risk: Delay allows competitor relationship building
```
